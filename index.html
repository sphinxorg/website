Core Mission

Why the World Needs
Post-Quantum Identity

Privacy is not only about money. Today the primary threat is identity manipulation — AI systems can replicate faces, synthesize voices, generate fake videos, and simulate entire personalities without permission.

01
Hash-Based Cryptography

When quantum machines break RSA and ECC it threatens to both humanity and the digital realm, SPIF address Built on hash-based signatures to cryptographically remain secure.

02
Universal Sovereign Identity

The fingerprint is a sovereign digital identity layer — not limited to cryptocurrency. It can bind authorship, ownership, communication, and authentication to any type of raw data.

03
Universal Proof of Humans

In a world where AI can fake your face, steal your voice, and duplicate your signature — a valid cryptographic signature tied to a post-quantum key cannot be simulated without the private key.

04
Open Source Defense

The defense of humanity cannot be proprietary. In the era of AI and quantum disruption, protection must be transparent, verifiable, and owned by everyone. Open code means collective security.

"The fingerprint stands as humanity's final stronghold — as the battle between creator and creation advances without restraint. Universal proof of digital sovereignty is no longer optional — it is essential."

A face can be faked. A voice can be cloned. A signature can be forged. But a valid cryptographic signature tied to a post-quantum key cannot be simulated without the private key.

"Privacy must evolve beyond economic participation — it must protect digital identity itself. The fingerprint becomes a sovereign identity layer resistant to forgery, resistant to AI replication."

Control over authorship. Control over attribution. Control over representation. Control over digital presence. Privacy is the ability to prove what is genuinely yours — and to reject what is not.

Architecture & Stack

Sphinx Protocol is implemented in Go with a PBFT-based consensus engine, VDF-based randomized leader election, and post-quantum cryptographic primitives throughout the stack.

Sign Any Digital Asset

Post-quantum signature attached to any raw data — JPEG, PNG, PDF, DOCX, MOV, MP4, and more. The signature links the file irrevocably to your SPIF address, creating verifiable proof of authorship, ownership, and origin that cannot be forged or replicated.

Individual Photographers, videographers, writers, and artists can sign their work at creation time — proving authorship against AI forgeries, deepfakes, and content theft.
Business Sign contracts, legal tenders, invoices, and compliance documents with verifiable post-quantum signatures. Eliminate document fraud and provide court-admissible authorship proof.

Encrypt & Protect Local Data

Post-quantum keys to encrypt any local data — files, folders, databases, archives. The encrypted payload can be stored anywhere: cloud drives, USB drives. Only your key can decrypt it, even against future quantum-capable adversaries.

Individual Encrypt personal photos, medical records, financial documents, and private journals. Store them anywhere without trusting the storage provider — only you hold the key.
Business Protect proprietary source code, client data, trade secrets, and internal records. Achieve compliance-grade encryption (GDPR, HIPAA) that survives the quantum transition.
Store Anywhere Zero Trust SPHINCS+ Signed

Business Share sensitive contracts, M&A data, strategic plans, and client communications over encrypted channels with post-quantum forward secrecy. Zero reliance on third-party providers.

Coins Overview

SPX Coins

The Sphinx Protocol coins (SPX) powers staking, governance, and sovereign identity registration across the network. ICO details will be announced when the testnet milestone is reached.

SPX Overview

SPX is the native currency of the Sphinx Protocol. The base unit is nSPX (nano SPX) — the smallest denomination. 1 SPX equals 1e18 nSPX, similar to how 1 Ether equals 1e18 wei.
